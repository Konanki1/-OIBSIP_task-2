A. P. J Abdul kalam

1931 - 2015

Miss Man Of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abudul Kalam15 October 1931,in Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu,was an eminent Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in developing the country's missile and space programs,Kalam was instrumental in the success of the SLV-III project,the Agni and Prithvi missiles,and the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program.After his presidency, he dedicated himself to education and inspiring the youth,becoming a visiting professor and authoring several influential books.His visionary ideas for India's development,simplicity, and humility earned him widespread respect and the Bharat Ratna,India’s highest civilian honor, in 1997.He passed away on July 27 2015,while delivering a lecture,leaving behind a legacy of scientific achievement and a vision for a progressive India.He known as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India," was a beacon of inspiration and innovation whose life journey epitomized dedication and excellence. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a pioneering scientist and the 11th President of India. Dr. Kalam's monumental contributions to India's space and missile programs,including the successful deployment of the Rohini satellite and the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles,laid the groundwork for the nation’s advancements in defense and aerospace technology. His tenure as President from 2002 to 2007 was marked by his humility,simplicity and unwavering commitment to education and youth empowerment.Through his writings and speeches, he inspired millions to dream big and work diligently towards a developed India.Dr. Kalam's legacy is a testament to his belief in the power of dreams, hard work, and integrity, leaving an indelible mark on the nation and its future generations.
